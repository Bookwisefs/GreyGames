⚄ Dice Chess

How to Play

🎲 The Basics

Each turn you roll 2 dice. You must move pieces whose point values add up to exactly the dice total.
Piece values: Pawn=1, Knight=3, Bishop=3, Rook=5, Castle=5, Queen=9. King uses your entire turn.

♟ Moving Pieces

You can move multiple pieces per turn as long as their values sum exactly to your roll. Example: roll 8 = Rook(5) + Knight(3).
Captures are free — taking an enemy piece costs no extra points.
You cannot move the same piece twice in one turn.

♔ The King

The King is always a valid choice, but only before you move any other piece. Moving the King ends your turn.
Check is not enforced — you can leave your King in danger or move into check. But if your opponent rolls the right amount to capture it... you lose!
If you can't make the exact total with non-King pieces, you must move the King. If the King has no moves either — Stalemate, you lose!

👑 Winning

Capture the enemy King to win. The King can only be captured if it uses your last point that turn.

♟ Pawn Promotion

When a pawn reaches the last rank, you choose what to promote it to: Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

🏰 Castling

Castling costs 5 points like a Rook move. You can still spend remaining points after castling.
